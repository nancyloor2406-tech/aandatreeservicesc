Frequently Asked Questions

1. How much does the average tree trimming service cost?

The tree trimming service cost varies based on the size of the tree, its location, and the complexity of the job. Generally, smaller trees or routine pruning are more affordable, while large, hazardous removals require more equipment and labor. We offer free, transparent estimates to provide you with an accurate price for your specific needs.

2. When is the best time of year for apple tree trimming?

For most regions in the U.S., apple tree winter pruning is ideal. Trimming while the tree is dormant reduces stress on the plant and makes it easier to see the structure of the branches, allowing for more precise cuts that promote better fruit production in the spring.

3. Do you provide tree services in my specific city?

Yes! A & A Tree Services is a nationwide provider. We work with a network of local tree trimmers in my area across all 50 states, from major metros like New York City and Los Angeles to smaller suburban and rural communities.

4. Is tree stump removal included with a tree removal service?

While tree removal and tree stump removal are often requested together, they are typically two different processes. Removing the tree involves cutting it down to a low stump, whereas stump removal involves specialized grinding equipment. We can bundle these services together for a more comprehensive and affordable solution.

5. What is the difference between tree trimming and pruning?

Tree trimming is usually focused on aesthetics and thinning out overgrown branches to improve curb appeal. Tree pruning service is more focused on the health and safety of the tree, involving the removal of diseased, dead, or structurally weak limbs to ensure long-term growth.

6. Are your tree care professionals insured?

Absolutely. Safety is our top priority. All of our tree care specialists and local tree trimming services are fully insured and bonded. This protects both our workers and your property during any tree cutting or removal project.
